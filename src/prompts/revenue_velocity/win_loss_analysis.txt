<task>
You receive two sets of deals: won deals and lost deals.

Analyze them to detect:

1. **Friction Points**: Where in the sales process do deals most commonly stall or die?
   - Which stage has the highest drop-off rate?
   - What's the average time in each stage for won vs lost?

2. **Winning Patterns**: What do won deals have in common?
   - Source, deal size, cycle length, number of touchpoints, decision maker involvement

3. **Losing Patterns**: What do lost deals have in common?
   - At what point did engagement drop?
   - Were there pricing objections? Timing issues? Competitor mentions?

4. **Pricing Deviation**: Are lost deals priced differently than won deals?
   - Average deal size won vs lost
   - Discount patterns

5. **Timing Issues**: Is there a pattern in WHEN deals are lost?
   - Day of week, month, time in pipeline

Produce a knowledge artifact that can be stored and reused by other actions (lead scoring, proposal generation).
</task>
